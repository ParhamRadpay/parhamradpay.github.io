Parham Radpay

PhD in mathematical physics • CEA and Université Paris-Saclay

About

I am a researcher in mathematical physics working at the interface of statistical physics, probability, and quantum many-body theory. My work focuses on disordered quantum systems, random tensors, and non-perturbative renormalization-group methods, with the goal of understanding complexity and phase transition in high-dimensional tensorial models.

Research interests

  • Functional renormalization group
  • Quantum spin glasses
  • Random tensors, random matrices and high-dimensional probability
  • Signal detection for tensorial data and statistical physics methods for machine learning
